## PACING OBSERVATIONS

- **Part III is the heaviest section** by word count and emotional weight. Three consecutive chapters (14, 15, 16) at the upper range risk reader fatigue. Consider whether any of the surrounding chapters (13, 17) can serve as deliberate breathers---shorter, quieter, giving the reader room before the next blow.

- **Part IV is the shortest section** and the only one with 5 chapters instead of 6. This creates natural acceleration toward the end, which works---the reader should feel momentum carrying them through the ceremony and into the reception.

- **Part II has one potential sag.** Ch. 8 (Public School) and Ch. 11 (The Attention) are both on the shorter side and risk feeling thin next to the richer chapters around them. Ensure each earns its place with a specific, memorable scene rather than general atmosphere.

- **The final chapter should resist expansion.** Ch. 27 will be tempting to overwrite---the metaphor, the resolution, the final image. But the power of the ending depends on restraint. 2,500--3,000 words is likely the sweet spot.

- **Chapter 1 is currently drafted at ~12,000 words.** This is 3--4x the target range. Significant cutting or restructuring will be needed to bring it in line with the planned chapter lengths.
